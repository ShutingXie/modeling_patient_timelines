# Modeling Patient Timelines: Forecasting Future Conditions

## Task Introduction

This task tests how well you can model longitudinal electronic health records (EHR). A
patient's history is a stream of timestamped clinical events: diagnoses, medications,
procedures, and labs. What we are testing is your ability to turn that multi-table,
multi-modal history into a representation a model can learn from, then evaluate it fairly on
patients it has never seen.

We provide a fully synthetic EHR dataset generated by Synthea. For each patient we define a
single landmark called the **anchor**: the date five years before that patient's last recorded
encounter. Using only events before the anchor, you predict what happens after:

- **Future conditions (multi-label).** Predict which clinical conditions are **newly
  diagnosed** in the five years after the anchor. A condition counts only if its first-ever
  diagnosis falls in that window; conditions the patient already had before the anchor do not
  count.

We recommend a patient-timeline sequence model: a transformer trained on tokenized event
sequences, pretrained on the timeline and then fine-tuned for the task. A decoder-only
transformer with a next-event objective is a good starting point, but the modeling approach is
your choice. How you design the representation is up to you, including the vocabulary, how you
encode time, and which fields and modalities you use. You may use any modality provided
(structured CSV, imaging, genomics). However, the structured CSV tables are the primary source and are enough to complete the task.

You submit predictions on a held-out test set whose future is withheld, and we score them
against the hidden ground truth.

## Dataset

The data is fully synthetic (Synthea). Every patient has a stable `Id` that links all tables
and modalities. We provide the structured EHR data train, validation, and test splits; do not make your own.  Un-split
genomics (DNA) and imaging are also provided as optional enrichment.

- **Train and validation** patients are released in full. Compute each patient's anchor
  yourself: take their last recorded encounter from `encounters.csv` (the latest `STOP`, or
  `START` if `STOP` is blank) and subtract five years. Truncate the timeline at the anchor, and
  build labels from the conditions **newly diagnosed** in the five years after it (first-ever
  diagnosis in the window). Avoiding leakage across the anchor is part of what we assess.
- **Test** patients are released already truncated at the anchor, so only pre-anchor events are
  included. We give you each test patient's anchor date in `test_anchors.csv`. Their outcomes
  are withheld. Score every test patient we give you.
- Validation is for monitoring and model selection only. Do not train on validation or test.

### Folder Structure

```
patient_splits.csv      # patient Id -> split (train | val | test)
target_conditions.csv   # the condition codes that are scored
test_anchors.csv        # anchor date per test patient (Id, anchor_date)

train_val/              # full CSV tables for train + val patients
test/                   # same CSV tables for test patients, truncated at each anchor
genomics/               # genomic files, un-split (optional)
imaging/                # imaging studies, un-split (optional)
```

The `genomics/` and `imaging/` files are named by patient `Id` (`<Id>_dna.csv` and
`<Id>.dcm`), so you join them to a split through `patient_splits.csv`. Each is optional and
covers only a subset of patients.

## Submission

Submit four things:

1. `your_name_results.pdf`: the technical report described below.
2. A public **GitHub** repository with your code, so we can reproduce your pipeline.
3. Your trained model on the **Hugging Face** Hub, so we can reproduce your evaluation.
4. `predictions.csv`: probabilities (not hard labels) for every test patient, one column per
   target condition. Use the condition **code** from `target_conditions.csv` as the column
   header (not the description), so we can match your columns when scoring.

```
predictions.csv
patient_id,444814009,195662009,10509002, ...   # one column per code in target_conditions.csv
```

### Report contents

1. **Representation.** What is one unit of input? Which fields and modalities did you use, and
   why? How did you handle continuous values such as lab results, and the time between events?
2. **Model and training.** Architecture, objective, hyperparameters, and your wandb training
   and validation curves.
3. **AI workflow.** How you used AI coding tools.
4. **Interpretation.** Why your model does well or poorly across the different conditions.

### Requirements

- Track training with wandb, and include a link to your wandb run in the report.
- On the test set, report macro-averaged AUROC and mean average precision (mAP) across the
  target conditions. Report any other metrics you think are important.
- Use of AI coding assistants is expected and encouraged. Describe your workflow.
- Prioritize a correct, leak-free, well-evaluated pipeline over a large or clever model.
- Do not use external datasets or pretrained weights. Train only on the provided training split.
